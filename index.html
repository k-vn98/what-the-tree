The agency that provided this information is Trees Count! 2015 it was a volunteer-powered campaign to map, count, and care for all of New York City's street trees, involving more than 2,200 volunteers. Citizen mappers used high-tech tools to create a spatially accurate digital inventory of NYC's street trees.

This dataset is being used because trees are cool.

This dataset is important because it will help support critical advances in municipal urban forest management and volunteer engagement.

On the Info Page you will find information such as where they are located or what species they are. The Analaysis page will show a graph that has numbers such as how much trees are in each borough.
